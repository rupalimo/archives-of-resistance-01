Cintia Anahi Pili read by Belén

Marcela me ofreció un techo, un amor, junto con ella comencé una nueva vida. La pérdida de Marcela fue muy fuerte para mí. Ella me enseñó TODO, a ser mujer, a usar tacos, a caminar, a maquillarme. Estamos hablando de la década de los noventa, era una década compleja, había edictos policiales que nos criminalizaban en la que estaba regido el artículos 92 inciso B y el artículo 68. Yo en ese momento era menor, andaba con un documento falso para que no me llevaran detenida, igual me llevaban detenida pero nunca decía que era menor de edad. Lamentablemente transité por el camino de la prostitución. Fue una experiencia dolorosa pero también una experiencia que me enseñó muchas cosas, me enseñó a subsistir, a compartir. Caímos detenidas varias compañeras, en ese momento compartimos los pocos que teníamos. Las detenciones en Mar del Plata duraban entre 5 y 30 días.

Para toda persona los afectos son importantes, el afecto de la familia siempre fue tan importante. Uno siempre necesita de la familia, por más peleada que esté. Mi madre es mi compañera de vida, mi confidente. La extrañaba mucho, extrañaba mucho a mis hermanos. Así que un dia dije necesito de ellos y una Navidad les caí a mi familia como Cintia, les toque el timbre y mi madre me abrió la puerta, me dice “cómo es tu nombre ahora” “soy Cintia mamá”, me dice “ahora sos otra persona, ahora sos mi Cintia” y me quebré a llanto en sus brazos, ella me abrazó como siempre lo hizo. Fue un momento muy importante en mi vida, fue muy emotivo, ahí entendí que mi familia no tenía ningún problema en aceptarme como Cintia. Mi recuerdo más valioso fue esa Navidad cuando mi familia abrió las puertas y me aceptaron como soy, ahí me demostraron el amor verdadero.

Uno de los mejores valores de la amistad me parece que es estar presentes. En esos momentos donde estás pasando por una situación difícil, es donde más tenemos que estar presentes.

Mi mayor logro ha sido mi documento con mi verdadera identidad, gracias a la lucha de muchas compañeras, esas compañeras que nos han dejado de este mundo, sin su lucha nosotras no podríamos seguir adelante.

English Translation:

Marcela gave me a roof and love. I started a new life with her. The loss of Marcela was very hard for me. She taught me EVERYTHING, to be a woman, to wear heels, to walk, and to put on makeup. We are talking about the nineties, it was a complex decade. A police order criminalized us through article 92, subsection B, and article 68. At that time I was a minor. I was carrying a false ID so that if they detained me, I would be detained but at least they didn’t know I was a minor. Unfortunately, I went down the path of prostitution. It was a painful experience but also an experience that taught me many things, taught me to subsist, to share. Several friends were arrested. At that time, we shared what little we had. The arrests in Mar de Plata lasted between 5 and 30 days.

Everyone needs affection and care. It’s very important. For me, the care of my family has always been so important. One always needs a family, no matter how hard it is. My mother is my life partner, my confidant. I missed her so much. I missed my brothers so much. So one day, I decided that I needed them. One Christmas, I went to see my family as Cintia. I rang the bell and my mother opened the door for me, she asked "What is your name now?" "I'm Cintia, mom.” She told me "Now you are another person. You are my Cintia now.” I bursted into tears in her arms. She hugged me as she always did. It was a very important moment in my life. It was very emotional. There, I understood that my family had no problem accepting me as Cintia. My most valuable memory was that Christmas when my family opened the doors and accepted me as I am. They showed me, true love.

For me, being present is the most valuable aspect ​​of a friendship. In moments when we are going through a difficult situation, it is most important to be present.

My greatest achievement has been my ID with my true identity, thanks to the struggle of many friends, those who have parted from this world. Without their fight, we could not continue.
